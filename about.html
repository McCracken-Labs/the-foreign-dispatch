About

How this is made — and how to read it

The Foreign Desk exists to answer one question: what is the rest of the world actually saying about the United States? Not filtered through American commentary, but in the words of each country's own press. Every morning it gathers top US-related headlines from newspapers around the world, translates them into English, and links straight to the original article.

How to read the leaning tags

No outlet is neutral, and pretending otherwise would defeat the purpose. Each source carries a tag so you can weigh its framing:

Independent / neutral State-funded, broad editorial Pro-government State-controlled

Independent outlets are privately owned with editorial independence. State-funded, broad editorial (e.g. Al Jazeera, France 24) are publicly financed but carry a wide range of views. Pro-government outlets reliably reflect their government's line without being formally controlled. State-controlled outlets (e.g. RT, Global Times) are direct instruments of state messaging — still worth reading, precisely because they show what a government wants the world to believe.

How sources rotate

To keep things fresh and give you a genuinely global picture over time, the homepage features a rotating slice of roughly nine to twelve countries drawn from a larger pool. Whichever countries are central to the day's biggest US story are always included; the rest rotate. Every edition is preserved in the Archive.

An honest limitation

Some major Western outlets — the BBC, The Guardian, Le Monde, Der Spiegel, the Times of India — block the automated tool this site uses to read the web. Where possible their absence is filled with other credible outlets from the same region, but it does mean today's accessible pool leans somewhat toward state-funded and Global-South media. This is disclosed rather than hidden. A future upgrade to a licensed news feed would close the gap.

A note on translation

Non-English headlines are translated to convey meaning and tone, not word-for-word. Where the original matters, it is shown beneath the translation. For anything consequential, follow the link and read the source.
